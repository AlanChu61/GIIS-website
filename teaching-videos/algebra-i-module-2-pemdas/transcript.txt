The fight goes on for days. The wild part? Both groups know how to do arithmetic. The thing they're missing is order of operations. Today we make sure you're never on the wrong side of that argument.

Three things to cover. One — the rule, PEMDAS, and what each letter means. Two — why the rule even exists. Three — four worked examples plus a try-it-yourself. By the end, ambiguous internet math problems will be obvious to you.

PEMDAS. P is for parentheses, do these first. E is for exponents, do these next. M and D — multiplication and division — share the same priority, do them left to right. A and S — addition and subtraction — also share priority, left to right. So really there are four levels, not six. P, then E, then MD together, then AS together.

Why do we need this rule? Because without it, the same expression has multiple answers. Take six plus four times three. Without the rule, going left to right gives ten times three, which is thirty. With the rule, you do four times three first to get twelve, then add six to get eighteen. Eighteen versus thirty — that's a huge gap from one expression. PEMDAS picks one and makes everyone agree.

Example one. Six plus four times three. No parentheses, no exponents. Multiplication beats addition. So four times three first, that's twelve. Then six plus twelve equals eighteen. Done.

Example two. Twelve divided by two plus four. Same priority decision. Division beats addition. Twelve divided by two is six. Six plus four is ten.

Example three. Two squared plus five. Exponents come before addition. Two squared is four. Four plus five is nine.

Example four. Five times the quantity three plus two, minus four. Parentheses first. Three plus two is five. Then five times five is twenty-five. Then minus four is twenty-one. Notice — even though we have multiplication and subtraction, we still did parentheses first.

Your turn. Try this one. Eight plus three times two squared. Pause and solve. Remember the order — exponents, then multiplication, then addition.

Time. Two squared is four. Three times four is twelve. Eight plus twelve is twenty. Twenty. If you got fifty-six you forgot exponent has higher priority than multiplication. If you got sixteen you forgot exponent. Twenty is correct.

Two trap questions to avoid. One — multiplication and division share priority. Don't always do multiplication first. Eight divided by four times two — do them left to right, divide first, then multiply. Two times two equals four, not one. Two — same with addition and subtraction. Ten minus three plus two is nine, not five. Left to right within the same level.

Recap. PEMDAS — Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division left to right, Addition and Subtraction left to right. Four levels of priority, not six. Always work top down. Inside a level, go left to right. That's the whole rule, and it never breaks.
